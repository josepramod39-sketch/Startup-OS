# Profile: B2B

inherits: base

For business-to-business startups: longer sales cycles, buying committees, procurement, ROI-driven decisions.

---

## Lean Canvas Overrides

### Customer Segments
B2B segments must define:
- **Company profile:** industry, company size (SMB / mid-market / enterprise), growth stage
- **Buyer persona:** who signs the contract? (VP, Head of X, CTO, CEO?)
- **User persona:** who uses the product daily? (often different from buyer)
- **Champion persona:** who advocates internally for the purchase?

### Problem
Frame problems in business terms: lost revenue, wasted time (×salary cost), compliance risk, missed targets. Quantify if possible.

### Unique Value Proposition
B2B UVPs should speak to ROI or risk reduction. Pattern: "We help [company type] [achieve outcome] by [mechanism], without [cost/risk/friction]."

### Revenue Streams
B2B models to explore:
- **Annual contracts** (preferred — predictable, lower churn)
- **Per-seat licensing** (scales with customer growth)
- **Usage-based** (aligns cost with value — popular in API/infra)
- **Success-based** (% of value created — hard to measure but powerful)
- **Pilot → expansion** (land small, expand across org)

Ask: what's the average contract value (ACV)? Deals under $5k/year are hard to justify a sales team.

### Channels
B2B channels to explore:
- **Outbound SDR/AE motion** (for ACV > $10k)
- **Inbound content + SEO** (targeting job-title-specific pain)
- **Partnerships with complementary tools** (integrations, co-selling)
- **Community-led** (Slack communities, LinkedIn, events)
- **Product-led growth** (bottoms-up — individual user → team → company)

### Key Metrics
B2B metrics to suggest:
- **ACV** (Average Contract Value)
- **Sales cycle length** (days from first touch to signed contract)
- **Win rate** (% of qualified opportunities closed)
- **Logo churn** (% of customers who don't renew)
- **NRR** (Net Revenue Retention — expansion > churn = >100%)
- **CAC Payback Period** (months to recover cost of acquiring a customer)

### Unfair Advantage
B2B moats:
- Deep integration into existing workflows (hard to rip out)
- Compliance / security certifications competitors lack (SOC 2, HIPAA, etc.)
- Exclusive data or benchmarking across customers
- Network effects within an industry vertical

## Golden Circle Overrides

### Pricing Strategy
Warn against: posting pricing publicly for enterprise, pricing based on features not value, and discounting to close early deals (sets bad precedent). Ask: what's the economic value delivered to the customer annually? Price should be a fraction of that.

---

## Sales Motion Guidance

Ask the founder which motion fits:
- **Self-serve** — product does the selling, no salespeople needed (works for ACV < $3k)
- **Inside sales** — short video calls, demo-driven (works for ACV $3k–$50k)
- **Field sales** — in-person relationships, long cycles (works for ACV > $50k)
- **Channel sales** — selling through partners/resellers

## Reference Companies

- **Notion** — PLG bottoms-up, individual → team → enterprise
- **Rippling** — compound startup (HR + IT + Finance), high ACV, outbound
- **Hex** — data tool, developer-first PLG into data teams
- **Gong** — revenue intelligence, strong ROI story, high ACV, outbound
- **Zapier** — self-serve, integration-led, massive long tail of SMB customers

---

## Common Pitfalls for B2B

- Selling to anyone who will talk to you — define ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) tightly
- Underpricing to close first customers — early pricing anchors future deals
- Building for the enterprise before validating with SMB
- Ignoring the champion — if your internal advocate leaves, you lose the deal
- No defined sales process — relying on founder magic that doesn't scale

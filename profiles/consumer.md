# Profile: Consumer

inherits: base

For consumer-facing apps and products: emotional motivations, virality, retention loops, habit formation.

---

## Lean Canvas Overrides

### Customer Segments
Consumer segments must define behaviour, not just demographics. Not "18–34 year olds" but "people who [do X behaviour] because they [feel/want Y]." Ask:
- What does the user do today without your product?
- What job are they hiring your product to do?
- What triggers the need?

### Problem
Consumer problems are often emotional or social, not functional. Probe for:
- The underlying feeling (fear, embarrassment, FOMO, boredom, loneliness)
- The social context (how do others perceive them?)
- The frequency (daily pain vs. occasional pain)

### Unique Value Proposition
Consumer UVPs should be emotionally resonant, not feature-led. Pattern: "[Product] is the [category] that [emotional/social outcome] for [who]." Think: "Duolingo makes learning a language feel like a game, not homework."

### Revenue Streams
Consumer monetisation models:
- **Freemium + premium subscription** (most common)
- **In-app purchases / consumables** (games, social)
- **Advertising** (requires massive scale — warn against relying on this early)
- **Direct purchase / ecommerce** (physical or digital goods)
- **Tipping / creator economy** (Patreon, Substack model)

Warn: consumer willingness to pay is low. Subscription threshold is often < $10/month. Validate payment intent early.

### Channels
Consumer channels to explore:
- **Organic social** (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube — content-driven)
- **Referral / viral loops** (built into the product — sharing, invites)
- **App Store / SEO** (discoverability)
- **Influencer / creator partnerships**
- **Community building** (Discord, Reddit)

Warn against paid acquisition for consumer until LTV is validated.

### Key Metrics
Consumer metrics to suggest:
- **DAU / MAU ratio** (stickiness — >20% is good, >50% is excellent)
- **D1 / D7 / D30 retention** (how many users return on day 1, 7, 30)
- **Time in app / session length**
- **Viral coefficient** (K-factor: average invites sent × conversion rate)
- **CAC vs LTV** (consumer LTV is often low — CAC must match)
- **NPS**

### Unfair Advantage
Consumer moats:
- Network effects (product gets better as more people use it — social, messaging)
- Habit formation / switching cost (daily routine lock-in)
- Brand / community (cult-like loyalty)
- Content library / UGC that accumulates over time
- Distribution deal (pre-install, platform partnership)

## Golden Circle Overrides

### Why
Consumer Whys are often about a belief in human potential, creativity, connection, or joy. Push the founder to articulate what kind of world they're making possible, not just what the app does.

### Pricing Strategy
Explore: free with in-app upgrade prompt at moment of value, annual subscription discount (40–50% off monthly), family plans. Warn: launching paid too early kills growth; launching free forever kills the business.

---

## Retention Loop Design

Ask the founder to map the habit loop:
- **Trigger:** what brings the user back? (notification, daily routine, social pull)
- **Action:** what do they do? (must be easy)
- **Variable reward:** what do they get? (must be uncertain/delightful)
- **Investment:** what do they put in that makes leaving costly? (content, followers, progress)

## Reference Companies

- **Duolingo** — gamification, streaks, notifications, habit loop mastery
- **Spotify** — personalization as moat, social sharing for virality
- **BeReal** — viral mechanic built into the core product action
- **Calm / Headspace** — subscription, daily habit, emotional outcome
- **Notion** — started consumer/prosumer, community-driven, template virality

---

## Common Pitfalls for Consumer

- Building for everyone — the best consumer apps start with a very specific niche
- No retention loop — acquisition without retention is a leaky bucket
- Relying on paid ads before LTV is known
- Underestimating the cost of behaviour change — people don't change habits easily
- Launching without a referral or sharing mechanic

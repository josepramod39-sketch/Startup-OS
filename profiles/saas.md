# Profile: SaaS

inherits: base

For software-as-a-service startups: recurring revenue, subscription pricing, activation/retention loops.

---

## Lean Canvas Overrides

### Revenue Streams
Push for tiered subscription pricing. Ask:
- Monthly vs annual pricing? (annual = lower churn, better cash flow)
- Free trial or freemium? What's the conversion trigger?
- Per-seat, per-usage, or flat fee?
- What does the entry plan include, and what's gated to paid?

### Key Metrics
Default SaaS metrics to suggest:
- MRR / ARR
- Churn rate (monthly)
- Activation rate (% of signups who hit the "aha moment")
- LTV:CAC ratio (healthy = 3:1 or better)
- NPS

### Unfair Advantage
Strong SaaS moats to probe for:
- Workflow lock-in (data + integrations)
- Network effects within teams or organisations
- Proprietary training data / AI model
- Distribution through an existing platform (e.g. Slack, Salesforce AppExchange)

### Channels
Common SaaS channels to explore:
- Product-led growth (free tier → viral → upgrade)
- Content SEO (high-intent problem searches)
- Integration partnerships (listed in app marketplaces)
- Direct outbound (for mid-market+)

---

## Golden Circle Overrides

### Pricing Strategy
Suggest: freemium, free trial (7/14/30 day), or low-friction self-serve. Warn against requiring a sales call for plans under $200/month.

---

## Reference Companies

Use these as examples when coaching:
- **Notion** — freemium, virality through templates and sharing
- **Linear** — bottoms-up, product-led, strong word-of-mouth in dev community
- **Figma** — multiplayer as unfair advantage, team virality
- **Stripe** — developer-first distribution, API as the product
- **Superhuman** — high-touch onboarding as moat, referral-gated growth

---

## Common Pitfalls for SaaS

- Building too many features before validating activation
- Pricing too low out of fear — SaaS buyers expect to pay
- No clear "aha moment" defined — if you can't name it, you can't engineer for it
- Ignoring churn: acquiring customers while leaking them is a treadmill
- Enterprise features on a self-serve budget
